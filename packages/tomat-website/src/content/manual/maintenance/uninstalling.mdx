Removing tomat is straightforward, and a later reinstall starts clean rather than
trying to reach a Core that is gone. Nothing is scattered across your system: the
Client is a single app, and the Core keeps all of its state under `~/.tomat`. You
take each part off your machine on its own, the ordinary way for your operating
system, or with the matching terminal command, which is also where you choose what
data to keep.

## Remove tomat

On the desktop, remove tomat the same way you remove any other program, one part
at a time:

- **Windows.** Open Settings > Apps (Add or remove programs) and remove the tomat
  Client and the tomat Core from the list.
- **macOS.** Drag the tomat Client to the Trash. The Core runs in the background
  with no app icon and has no separate uninstaller on macOS, so remove it with the
  terminal command in the next section.
- **Linux.** The Client is an AppImage: delete that file to remove it, or use the
  terminal command below. If you installed the Core from a `.deb` or `.rpm`, remove
  it with your package manager (`sudo apt remove` on Debian, `sudo dnf remove` on
  Red Hat); otherwise the terminal command below removes it.
- **Android.** Press and hold the tomat icon and choose Uninstall.

How much data this leaves depends on the part. Removing the **Core** (through its
terminal command, or its entry in Windows Add or remove programs and your Linux
package manager) stops its background service and removes its sessions and
memories. Removing the **Client** the native way above takes the app off but
leaves its data (your settings and the list of Cores you paired with) under
`~/.tomat`; the terminal command in the next section removes that too, and lets
you keep the settings with a flag. Either way a reinstall never gets stuck on a
local Core that is gone: on its next launch the Client automatically drops a paired
local Core whose Core is no longer installed on this device.

## Uninstall via terminal

The one-line command that installed each part can also remove it, which is handy
over SSH or on a machine with no desktop. It is also where you change what data is
kept. Pick your operating system for each part to get the exact command, then copy
it into a terminal.

Removing the **Client** always removes your paired cores, and removes your settings too unless you add `--keep-data`:

## What tomat leaves behind

Downloaded models are always kept, whichever way you remove tomat. They can be
many gigabytes and are shared across channels, so no removal step touches them. If
you want that space back, delete the `~/.tomat/models` folder by hand.

If you run tomat on more than one **channel**, each is a separate install with its
own state under `~/.tomat`, and you remove one channel at a time. The terminal
commands here act on the latest channel. See
[Updates and Channels](/manual/maintenance/updates-and-channels) for how channels
stay isolated.

<Aside type="caution">
  Removing either part deletes that data for good. There is no undo, so if you want to keep your
  settings, sessions, or memories for a later reinstall, add the keep-data option before you run it.
</Aside>

That is everything tomat leaves on your machine, and how to take it back. If you
are setting up again rather than leaving, head back to
[installing tomat](/manual/getting-started/installing).
