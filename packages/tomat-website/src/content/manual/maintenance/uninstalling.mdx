Removing tomat is straightforward, and by default it leaves your data in place so
a later reinstall picks up where you left off. Nothing is scattered across your
system: the Client is a single app, and the Core keeps all of its state under
`~/.tomat`. You take each part off your machine on its own, the ordinary way for
your operating system, or with the matching terminal command.

## Remove tomat

On the desktop, remove tomat the same way you remove any other program, one part
at a time:

- **Windows.** Open Settings > Apps (Add or remove programs) and remove the tomat
  Client and the tomat Core from the list.
- **macOS.** Drag the tomat Client to the Trash. The Core runs in the background
  with no app icon and has no separate uninstaller on macOS, so remove it with the
  terminal command in the next section.
- **Linux.** If you installed from a `.deb` or `.rpm`, remove it with your package
  manager: `sudo apt remove` on Debian, `sudo dnf remove` on Red Hat. If you
  installed the Client as an AppImage, delete that file, and remove the Core with
  the terminal command below.
- **Android.** Press and hold the tomat icon and choose Uninstall.

However you remove it, the defaults are the same. Taking off the **Client** leaves
your settings and the list of Cores you paired with in place, ready for a
reinstall. Taking off the **Core** stops its background service first, then removes
its sessions and memories. To keep the Core's data instead, or to also wipe the
Client's settings, use the terminal, where each part takes a flag for exactly that.

## Uninstall via terminal

The one-line command that installed each part can also remove it, which is handy
over SSH or on a machine with no desktop. It is also where you change what data is
kept. Pick your operating system for each part to get the exact command, then copy
it into a terminal.

Removing the **Client** keeps your settings unless you add `--purge`:

## What tomat leaves behind

Downloaded models are always kept, whichever way you remove tomat. They can be
many gigabytes and are shared across channels, so no removal step touches them. If
you want that space back, delete the `~/.tomat/models` folder by hand.

If you run tomat on more than one **channel**, each is a separate install with its
own state under `~/.tomat`, and you remove one channel at a time. The terminal
commands here act on the latest channel. See
[Updates and Channels](/manual/maintenance/updates-and-channels) for how channels
stay isolated.

<Aside type="caution">
  Removing the Core, or removing the Client with `--purge`, deletes that data for good. There is no
  undo, so be sure you have what you want before you run it.
</Aside>

That is everything tomat leaves on your machine, and how to take it back. If you
are setting up again rather than leaving, head back to
[installing tomat](/manual/getting-started/installing).
