The **General** group is where you tell tomat the basics about yourself, decide what
the agent is allowed to know about your surroundings, and set how the app behaves
when it starts. None of it changes what the agent can do; it changes who it is
talking to and what it can take for granted.

<SettingsPanelDemo group="general" />

## You and the agent

Set **Your Name** so the agent can address you, and the **Agent Name** to give the
persona you are talking to an identity of its own. You can also set the **Language**
the agent should reply in, which is a request it honors as well as the model allows
rather than a hard switch. These are small touches, but they are the difference
between a generic assistant and one that speaks to you the way you want.

## What tomat shares

A model knows nothing about your world unless you tell it, so the General group lets
you choose what context to pass along: your rough location, the current date and
time, and the kind of computer you are on. Each is a toggle, so you decide what the
agent gets to assume. Sharing the date and time lets it reason about "today"
correctly; sharing your location helps with anything local; leaving them off keeps
the agent to what is in the conversation alone.

## Launch and sessions

A few settings shape how the app comes up. **Start at Login** brings tomat up with
your machine so it is always there. **New Session on Launch** decides whether you
open into a fresh session or back into your last one, and **Save Sessions** controls
whether conversations are kept at all, for when you would rather they leave nothing
behind.

## How It Works

The details you choose to share do not live inside the model; they are gathered into
a short block of context that the Core places at the front of each turn, alongside
your [system prompt](/manual/conversation/system-prompt). That is why a toggle takes
effect immediately and why turning one off genuinely withholds the information: if
the date or your location is not in that block, the agent never receives it for that
message. The naming and language settings ride along the same way, as part of the
standing context the model reads before your words.
