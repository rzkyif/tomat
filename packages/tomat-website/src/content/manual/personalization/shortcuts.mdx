**Shortcuts** are keys that drive tomat without reaching for the mouse, and the most
useful ones work even when tomat is not the window you are looking at. They are set
under **Shortcuts** in Settings, and because they reach across your whole desktop,
they are a Client feature: they belong to this app on this machine.

## What you can bind

The shortcuts cover the actions you want at hand from anywhere:

- **Show or hide the window**, so tomat is one keypress away no matter what you are
  doing. This is the same key a [Push-to-Talk](/manual/speech/speech-to-text) tap
  uses to toggle the window.
- **Attach a file**, to start a message with something from disk without opening the
  app first.
- **Capture the screen**, or **capture a region of it**, to hand the agent what you
  are looking at as an image in a couple of keystrokes.

Pick the combinations that do not collide with the rest of your system; tomat holds
them for as long as it is running.

## How It Works

A shortcut that works from any window is a **global** shortcut, and that is a request
the app makes to your operating system: it asks the OS to notify it whenever that key
combination is pressed, even while another app is focused. The OS, not tomat, watches
the keyboard and forwards the event, which is why a global shortcut keeps working
when tomat is hidden, and also why two apps cannot hold the same combination at once.
When you change a binding, tomat releases the old combination and registers the new
one, so the change takes hold right away.
