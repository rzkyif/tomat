A **greeting** is a prompt tomat runs for you when the app starts, so you open to
something useful instead of a blank session. Where a
[scheduled prompt](/manual/conversation/scheduled-prompts) fires on a clock, a
greeting fires on launch, which makes it the right tool for whatever you want to
see first: a summary of the day ahead, an overnight digest, a reminder of where you
left off.

## Configuring greetings

Turn greetings on under **Greetings** in Settings and choose when they fire. The
choice is between every time the app starts, or only when it starts on its own at
login, which is the distinction that keeps a greeting from firing every time you
reopen a window you closed by hand.

You set the instruction tomat follows and the title its session takes, with the
current date and time filled in for you, so a greeting can say "what is on for
today" and mean the actual today. The result is an ordinary session: you read it,
reply, and carry on from there.

## How It Works

A greeting lives on the Client, because the Client is what knows when the app has
opened. On launch, once it has connected to a Core, the Client checks whether
greetings are on and whether this particular start matches your trigger, then sends
your greeting text as the opening message of a fresh session. The date and time are
substituted into that text right before it is sent, so the model receives the real
moment rather than a placeholder. From the Core's side it is an ordinary turn,
which is why a greeting can do anything a normal message can, including reaching for
tools and memories.
