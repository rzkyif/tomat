tomat can read the agent's replies aloud with **Text-to-Speech**. It is the
counterpart to [Voice Input](/manual/speech/voice-input): where one turns your
speech into a message, this turns the agent's answer into audio, so a conversation
can be entirely spoken if you want it to be. It is useful when your eyes or hands
are busy, or simply when you would rather listen than read.

<Aside type="note">
  Text-to-Speech runs locally, so replies are spoken on your machine and nothing is sent away to be
  voiced. (Voice Input can run locally too, but unlike it, Text-to-Speech has no External option: it
  is always on-device.)
</Aside>

## Turning it on

Enable Text-to-Speech under its group in Settings, then choose a model and a
voice. The model is the engine that produces the speech; the voice is the
particular sound it speaks in.

<SettingsPanelDemo group="tts" />

Each model offers its own set of voices, and some span several languages, so the
right model depends as much on the voice and languages you want as on raw quality.
Picking a model may need a one-time download, which tomat always asks about first
and never fetches in the background.

Once it is on, replies are spoken as they arrive, so spoken output pairs naturally
with [Voice Input](/manual/speech/voice-input) for a hands-free session: you talk,
the agent answers aloud, and you never touch the keyboard.
