Once you have chosen a model, two dials change how it answers from one message to
the next. You reach both, along with the model preset, from the quick controls
that sit with the input bar, so you can adjust them mid-conversation without
opening Settings.

<QuickModelBarDemo />

These are per-message choices, not a mode you commit to. You can turn thinking up
for a hard question and back down for a quick follow-up in the same session,
because each turn carries its own settings.

## Thinking

**Thinking** is how much the model reasons before it replies. Turn it off when you
want a fast, direct answer to something simple. Raise it when a question needs the
model to work through steps, weigh options, or check itself, the kind of problem
where a snap answer tends to be wrong.

More thinking is not free: it takes longer and spends more of the
[context window](/manual/core-concepts/sessions-and-context), since the reasoning
itself occupies the model's attention. The reasoning shows up as a **Thinking**
bubble above the reply, which you can expand to read.

## Creativity

**Creativity** controls how freely the model chooses its words. Keep it low, or
precise, when you want factual, repeatable answers, the kind where you would expect
the same question to produce nearly the same response. Raise it for brainstorming,
varied phrasing, or anything where you want range rather than consistency.

The two dials are independent and often pull in opposite directions: a careful
factual answer wants thinking up and creativity down, while a freewheeling idea
session wants the reverse. There is no single right setting; you tune to the task
in front of you.

<Aside type="tip">
  A good default is Thinking off and Creativity low: fast, consistent answers. Reach for the dials
  only when a particular message earns it.
</Aside>

A note on what these dials are: they are requests to the model, not guarantees.
Each model honors a level as well as it can, and two different models may respond
to the same setting differently. Treat them as steering, not switches, and judge
by the replies you get.
