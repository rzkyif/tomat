**Documents** are notes you keep in tomat for the agent to draw on. Unlike an
attachment, which belongs to a single message, a document lives on your core and
stays available across every session, so a reference you add once is there for
every future conversation. They are how you give the agent lasting context about
your work, your projects, or yourself.

## Relevant documents

You do not have to remember which note matters for a given question. As you write
a message, tomat finds the documents most related to it, lets the agent see their
summaries, and lets it read the full text on demand. The note surfaces itself when
it is relevant, and stays out of the way when it is not.

<RelevantDocumentsDemo />

The conversation shows which documents were surfaced, so a reply that leans on a
note tells you which one. When nothing matches, tomat can either stay quiet or show
that it looked and found nothing, whichever you prefer, so silence is never
ambiguous.

## Referencing a document

When you know exactly which document you want, you do not have to wait for it to be
ranked in. Type `@` and the document's name in the input bar to drop its full
content straight into your message, the same mention you use for snippets. That is
the difference between the two paths: retrieval is tomat finding a note for you,
while a mention is you naming one outright.

## Settings

Turn relevant documents on or off, and cap how many tomat offers at once, under
**Documents** in Settings. The cap is the main dial worth knowing: a higher one
surfaces more notes per message at the cost of more of the
[context window](/manual/core-concepts/sessions-and-context), while a lower one
keeps replies lean.
