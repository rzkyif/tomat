Everything you send to the agent starts in the input bar at the bottom of a
session. It is a plain text box that grows to fit a long message and sends on
Enter, but it is also where you pull in files, reference your saved knowledge, and
switch to dictation, so it is worth knowing what it can do.

<UserInputDemo />

A single line is the common case, but the bar expands as you type, so a longer,
multi-paragraph prompt stays fully visible while you write it. Pressing Enter
sends; you do not have to reach for a button. The quick controls that sit with the
bar let you adjust how the model answers without opening Settings, covered in
[Thinking and Creativity](/manual/tuning-replies/thinking-and-creativity).

## Attachments

You can give the agent more than text by attaching files and images to a message.
Pick a file from disk, drop in a screenshot, or capture a region of your screen,
and it stages above the bar so you can review the set, or remove something, before
you send.

<AttachmentListDemo />

What the agent does with an attachment depends on its kind. A document is passed
along as text the agent can quote and work from, which is the right way to ask
about a file's contents. An image is only read if your model supports vision; with
a text-only model, an attached image is along for the ride but cannot be seen.

## Mentions

When the thing you want to bring in is already saved in tomat, you do not have to
attach it by hand. Type `@` in the bar to reference a **snippet** or a
**memory** by name, and tomat drops its contents straight into your message
without your hands leaving the keyboard. This is the fast path for instructions
and notes you reuse often.

Snippets and memories each have their own page:
[Snippets](/manual/knowledge/snippets) for reusable text you trigger by name, and
[Memories](/manual/knowledge/memories) for notes the agent can also find on its
own.

## Talking instead of typing

If you would rather speak than type, the bar can show a microphone for **Voice
Input**, transcribing what you say into the same box. The transcription can run
locally, so your audio stays on your machine. Setting it up, and the ways
dictation can start, are covered in [Voice Input](/manual/speech/voice-input).
