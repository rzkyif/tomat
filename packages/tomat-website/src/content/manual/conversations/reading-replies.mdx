A reply arrives as a **bubble**, streamed in as the model produces it rather than
appearing all at once. Your messages sit on one side of the conversation and the
agent's on the other, so the back-and-forth reads at a glance. Watching a reply
stream is also useful feedback: you can tell the model is working, and stop reading
or interrupt as soon as you have what you need.

## Thinking

Some replies are preceded by a **Thinking** bubble. When the model reasons through
a problem before answering, tomat shows that reasoning as its own bubble above the
reply, with a note of how long the model spent. It starts collapsed, so it never
buries the answer, and you can expand it when you want to see how the model got
there.

<ReasoningTraceDemo />

Thinking is worth reading when an answer surprises you or you want to check the
model's work, and easy to ignore when you just want the result. How much the model
thinks is something you set per message from the quick controls, covered in
[Thinking and Creativity](/manual/tuning-replies/thinking-and-creativity).

## Tool runs and other steps

A reply is not always just text. When the agent uses a tool or consults your
documents, those steps appear inline in the conversation as it works, so the reply
is a record of what the agent actually did, not only its final words. That inline
output is its own topic, covered in
[Inline Results](/manual/conversations/inline-results).

## When something goes wrong

A turn can fail, most often because an External provider is unreachable or has
rate-limited you. When that happens, tomat shows the error in place of the reply,
with enough detail to tell what went wrong, rather than failing silently or
dropping you back to an empty session.

<ErrorMessageDemo />

Because the failed turn stays in the conversation, you can read the reason, fix
what it points to (switch providers, wait out a rate limit, check a setting), and
try again without losing the thread.
