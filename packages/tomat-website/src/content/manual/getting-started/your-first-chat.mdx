When you open tomat you land in a **session**: one conversation with the agent.
You type in the bar at the bottom, and each reply arrives in a **bubble** above
it. A session is the unit you work in, and tomat saves every one locally, so you
can leave and come back to it later.

<ChatDemo />

## Sending a message

Type your message and press Enter. The agent answers by streaming its reply into
a bubble as it goes, so you see words appear rather than waiting for a finished
block of text. If you set up a **Local** model, the whole exchange happens on your
machine, with nothing sent off the device.

Your first message does double duty: tomat reads it to name the session for you,
so the conversation picks up a title without you stopping to write one. You can
rename it later, or ask tomat to generate a fresh name once the topic has shifted.

## Following along

The bar across the top of a session shows its title and a running measure of the
**context window**, how much of the model's attention the conversation has used so
far. Watching it fill is how you know when a long session is getting close to its
limit.

<SessionBarDemo />

The agent's work is visible as it happens, not hidden behind a spinner. When the
model thinks before answering, a **Thinking** bubble appears first and reports how
long it spent; you can expand it to read the reasoning. If the agent runs a tool,
that step shows up inline too, so you can follow what it did and why.

## Starting a new session

Start a new session whenever you want a clean slate. A fresh session gives the
model an empty context, which is the right move once a conversation has drifted
from what you are now asking. Your old sessions stay saved, so nothing is lost by
moving on.

That is the whole loop: type, read the reply, and start fresh when you need to.
From here, [How tomat Works](/manual/core-concepts/how-tomat-works) explains the
pieces behind that loop, and
[Sessions and Context](/manual/core-concepts/sessions-and-context) goes deeper on
the context window and when to start over.
