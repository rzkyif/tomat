A **session** is a single conversation, with its own history and its own context.
It is the unit you work in: you start one, talk back and forth, and tomat keeps
the whole thing saved locally so you can leave it and return later. Every session
is independent, so what you said in one has no bearing on another unless you bring
it across yourself.

## The session bar

The bar across the top of a session carries two things you keep an eye on: its
title, and a running measure of the **context window**. The context window is how
much of the model's attention the conversation has used so far, growing as you and
the agent exchange more turns.

<SessionBarDemo />

tomat names a session for you from your first message, so you do not have to title
it before you start. If the conversation moves on to a different topic, you can
edit the title directly or ask tomat to regenerate it from the current contents.

## Context and when to start fresh

The context window is finite. Every model can only consider so many tokens at
once, and a session draws on that budget as it grows. When the conversation runs
long, the oldest turns crowd the window, and eventually the earliest ones fall out
of the model's view entirely. The bar's fill is your warning that you are
approaching that edge.

The fix is rarely to fight a crowded context. When what you are asking now has
drifted from how the session began, a fresh session gives the model a clean window
and your full prompt its undivided attention, instead of competing with stale
history. Starting over is cheap, and the old session stays saved if you need it.

How large that window is depends on the model and, for a Local model, on a setting
you control: a larger **Context Window** holds more of the conversation but uses
more memory. That trade is covered in
[Models and Providers](/manual/core-concepts/models-and-providers).

## Switching sessions

Open the session list to move between your saved sessions or to start a new one.
Each session keeps its own history and context, so switching away from one and
back loses nothing; you pick up exactly where you left it. This is what lets you
keep several lines of work going at once, one session per thread, rather than
piling everything into a single ever-growing conversation.
